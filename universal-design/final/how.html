How to Achieve Universal Design

Creating a universally designed website involves several key practices. Here's how you can start:

Step 1: Use Semantic HTML

Ensure that your HTML markup is semantically correct. This helps search engines, screen readers, and other technologies better understand the structure of your page.

Step 2: Make Your Site Keyboard Accessible

Ensure that all interactive elements (links, buttons, form fields) are accessible via keyboard navigation. Users should be able to tab through your site and interact with content using just the keyboard.

Step 3: Provide Alt Text for Images

Always provide descriptive alternative text for your images, which helps visually impaired users understand the context of the image.
